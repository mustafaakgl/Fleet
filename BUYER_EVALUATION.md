## 2. What would stop me from buying Fleet? (the dealbreakers, honestly)

## 10. Market size: how many companies would buy? (panel voice, not Klaus)

Honest structure, German market (DACH multiplies by ~1.35):

**Addressable universe (fleets 15вҖ“100 vehicles, DE):**
- Road transport/logistics (GГјterkraftverkehr, ~47вҖ“50k licensed firms, heavily skewed small): ~9,000вҖ“13,000 firms in the 15вҖ“100 band
- Courier/express/last-mile subcontractors (KEP): ~4,000вҖ“7,000

- Healthcare/ambulant care & patient transport (fleets exist but mostly pool-car needs, weaker fit for Einsatzplan economics): ~2,000вҖ“4,000 usable fits
- Trades/service/construction fleets (huge universe, generic-fit only вҖ” Fleetio/Vimcar territory): ~15,000вҖ“25,000 (low win-rate for you; ignore in planning)

**Realistic serviceable market for the *current* product thesis (transport, KEP, staffing, security): вүҲ 15,000вҖ“22,000 companies in Germany.**

**How many actually buy Fleet?** Penetration for a well-executed niche SaaS with seedвҶ’A funding, 5-year horizon, German sales motion: 1вҖ“4% of SAM is the honest historical band (Vimcar needed years and a single killer feature to pass 5,000+ customers in a *broader* market).

**Point estimate at 5 years: ~400вҖ“500 paying companies (вүҲ вӮ¬3вҖ“6M ARR at blended вӮ¬600вҖ“900/mo).**
**95% confidence interval: 120 to 1,300 companies.** Yes, that interval is wide вҖ” that is what 95% honestly means for a pre-revenue-motion product. The variance is *not* in the market (the pain is real and the universe is sufficient); it is in execution: tacho + DATEV shipped or not, three reference customers in year one or not, founder doing German outbound sales or not. With the trust items of В§6 unsolved, the realistic outcome is the *bottom* of that interval; with them solved and the money loop shipped, the middle is conservative.

Sanity check the other way: you need just **0.3% of the SAM** to be a вӮ¬3M ARR company. The market is not your risk. You are your risk.
